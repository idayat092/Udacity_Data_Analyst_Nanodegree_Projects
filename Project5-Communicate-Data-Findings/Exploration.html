Structure of the dataset¶

The dataset used for this project consists of various information on 891 passengers that were aboard the ship. The information include: Passenger id, Name, survival (Yes or No), Ticket class, Sex, Age, relatives/siblings, Cabin and Embarkment. The data can be found at Titanic Train Data

New columns were engineered to indicated passengers with Siblings, spouses, parent or child.

Key Notes:

  • Survival - 0 (No), 1 (Yes)
  • Pclass - Ticket Class (1,2,3) - (1st class, 2nd class, 3rd class)
  • Embarked - Port of Embarkation C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown, S = Southampton

My expectation for the exploration is that a higher proportion of youths will survive as they are expected to have the strength and be agile enough to fight for survival. Passengers with parents/siblings/spouses/children should survive more as those will serve as a drive/motivation for them. 1st class passengers might survive more as they may be given priority treatment in terms of rescue plans.

Summary of Findings from Univariate Exploration¶

  • Majority of the passengers on board were youths between the age 20 to 30 years, most of which were boarding third class.
  • There were more males on board then females
  • Majority of the passengers embarked from Southampton
  • Distribution of the Fare is unimodal and rightly skewed, with majority of the passengers paying between 5 to 25 pounds

The 0s and 1s for Survival were replaced with No and Yes, so as to avoid confusion while interpreting. The xscale was also limited to show Fare between 0 and 200 with small bin size, so as to get the detailed distribution.

Summary of Findings from Bivariate Exploration¶

  • Correlation between Age and Fair is positive, but very weak.
  • When Survival is compared with Age, Majority of the passengers that survived are between the ages 20 and 40 years. Same with those that did not survived. This is quite expected as that is the age range that forms majority of the passengers on the ship
  • A higher proportion of passengers in 1st class survived, compared to that of 2nd and 3rd class
  • A higher proportion of females survived, compared to males
  • Having siblings/spouse seems not to affect survival rate, because even passengers with siblings/spouses did not have a higher proportion of survival.
  • Passengers with parent/child have a slighly higher proprtion of survival.
